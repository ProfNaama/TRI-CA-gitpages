TRI-CA

Trials with Conversational Agents

A flexible web-based research platform for designing and running digital experiments with AI.

TRI-CA is an open-source research platform designed to support a wide range of experimental studies involving LLM-based conversational agents (CAs). It enables researchers to efficiently collect user data before, during, and after interactions with AI across diverse experimental designs in naturalistic settings. The platform is best understood as a baseline template: it ships with intentionally simple example prompts, tasks, and questions that researchers can fork, replace, and extend for their own study.

This website provides a comprehensive, step-by-step guide to configuring and deploying TRI-CA for your own experimental design (see the Researcher's Guide). It walks you through setting up human-AI interaction workflows, administering surveys, implementing randomized treatment assignment, and capturing participant interactions and responses in a structured database. A dedicated Developer's Guide is also included for those who wish to modify and enhance the system's functionality.

In practice, most researchers adapt the baseline template by editing the questions_bank.csv, experiment_desc.csv, and treatment_groups_config.csv files, along with the hidden_prompts_bank/ and user_tasks_bank/ folders. Those files control the questionnaire content, visible page text, treatment-specific prompts, and participant task instructions without requiring a full rewrite of the application.

Built on a modern Node.js architecture, TRI-CA integrates user interfaces, database infrastructure, and OpenAI capabilities into a seamless, all-in-one environment. Researchers can quickly deploy sophisticated experiments with automated group assignment, flexible configurations, and minimal technical overhead. By leveraging hidden prompts and naturalistic interactions, TRI-CA enables precise control over AI behavior while maintaining a smooth participant experience. All interactions are automatically logged and timestamped, generating rich, high-resolution data suitable for both quantitative and qualitative analysis.
